5 Tips to Protect Yourself from the RECESSION!

With a looming recession, are you doing everything you can to solidly your position within your team?

Maybe you’re put off by that question, but in truth, this is one of your stronger moves you could possibly make. Simply out, economic slowdowns can be temporary, but as Graham Stephan once said, “Upskilling makes you well positioned for sunny days.”

1 — Find out what skills you are lacking

For get started, do this:

  1. Take some time one evening and scribble down on paper or your tablet the skills you have.
  2. In another column list the skills they would be useful to pick up.
  3. The following night, pick one item on your new “wish” list and go find a video or tuturial and DO IT!
  4. The night after that, do another. Work your way down the list.

In a month’s time, you’ll have grown your skill set in a favorable direction.

For even more tips on upskilling that fit your current organization (or the one your shooting for), check out this video:

2 — Work on your code QUALITY

Learning new toolkits that support your current skills is one thing.

But there’s another skill NOT covered in any Uni class or bootstrap program. It’s called “code polishing”. While this skill doesn’t provide new functions or operational code, this practice can improve your efficiency.

One of the biggies in code development is our need to go back and read code we’ve written before. And if you’re code is an incoherent mess, then it becomes much HARDER to do so.

Adopt some of the practices found in the video below, and you can make yourself INCREDIBLY VALUES to ANY team!

3 — Look ma! No hand-run tests!

Something else that may get glossed over, if covered at all, is automated testing.

Indeed, more and more shops are adopting this, but the odds that this was covered in your university or boot strap program is slim. And if covered, may have been only mentioned briefly.

However, it’s one of those skills that will pay you back for years to come. So, why not give yourself an edge by deepening your skills there as well.

Considering how how widely automated has been adopted, I made an entire playlist devoted to it.

  • Pull up the playlist below.
  • Go to the one you know the LEAST about and WATCH it!
  • On another night (or during a lunch break), watch the NEXT one.

Once you finish this playlist, you’ll be a LOT more confident to run with automated testing OR EVEN INTRODUCE IT to your team!

NOTE: A developer that can SHOW a passing test suite is worth a FORTUNE to any manager!

4 — Making your ninja move

Buried deep in this article we now come to a slick, cool move you SHOULD be making.

Shhh! Don’t tell anyone. This is just between you and me, okay?

Now this may sound strange but one the FASTEST places to gain traction with your team is learning to query the database for data.

Why? It seems just about anyone can write a web controller and everyone and their cousin seems to have had a hand dabbling with JavaScript…

…but not EVERYONE groks databases and querying them for real data to populate those web forms.

Everyone around you, whether you’re surrounded by new hires or you’re vying for a position, can probably write a slick web control with some JS toolkit. That’s why YOU must pull the ninja move of being better at database queries!

5 — Acing the interview

All these skills are for naught if you don’t land that position, amirite?

That’s why you need to KNOW what hiring managers are LOOKING for! (And in recessionary times, even if you’re on a team, there is increased risk of being let go).

I’ve been involved in many hiring decisions. Trust me, you don’t want to get caught flat footed. Even if you’re on a team, you can learn what notes to start keeping so that you’re ready for your NEXT interview (which not happen when YOU want it!)

So watch this and see what TO do and what NOT to do!

I’ll wrap this article up with a warning.

Maybe you sensed it because I made no reference to security and locking down apps.

Security is a beast. For some it’s a passion. For others, it’s a completely different batch of studies and courses.

Unless you’re going to devote the time it takes to master security, you’re better off spending your present time on the other stuff mentioned in this thread. Yes, it’s beneficial for ALL software developers to grok security, but this article is about tactics to “upskill” yourself, quickly.

Security is a MAJOR time sink and something that would require SACRIFICING the other things mentioned.

So my super secret advice is to let one of your competitors be the person that excels at security while you succeed at the things listed above.
