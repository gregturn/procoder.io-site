6 Ways to Become a SPRING Coder

So you’ve heard that Spring is the way? In your eagerness to dive into the workforce as a full fledge coder, you want to get crackin’.

And then it hits you…how do you get started?

1 — Start from the source

Several years ago, the Spring team released a slew lunchtime-sized guides. They’re free and updated to the latest stable version of Spring Boot.

  • Head over to https://spring.io/guides
  • Pick one and go through it. They are designed to NOT require completing OTHER tutorials or guides.
  • The next day, do another.
  • Rinse, repeat.

The guides aren’t specific to any Spring project. Instead, they each focus on solving a problem. There are about 60, so in the span of a 2–3 months, your knowledge of Spring can be widened considerably!

2 — Start reading This Week in Spring

If you want to catch the latest, then visit https://spring.io/blog. Not only is chock full of official news, but there’s a weekly post from Josh Long a.k.a. starbuxman dubbed This Week in Spring.

Josh has been writing this column since he joined the Spring (right after me), and he hasn’t stopped.

In his weekly roundup, he has some of the hottest news not just from the Spring team, but the Spring community at large.

And in case you didn’t catch it, the Spring community is HUGE. Which leads to the next way to ramp up your skills as a Spring coder…

3 — Tune in to the latest from the Spring community

There are multiple sources where you can catch interviews, news, and other Spring stuff.

  • A Bootiful Podcast (Josh Long)
  • The Spring Boot Learning Podcast (me!)
  • Java Pub House (Freddy Guime)

UPDATE: I have since pivoted my podcast to the Pro Coder Show and am no longer Spring-specific.

Podcasts are fun and exciting. They have a certain amount of tech knowledge mixed in with entertaining banter.

And all these podcasts are fun. Check ’em out!

4 — Grab a book!

Podcasts and news articles are great, but sometimes you need the information chained together with coding examples. And that’s where books really shine.

To get some of the latest check out:

  • Spring Boot: Up & Running (Mark Heckler)
  • Spring in Action (Craig Walls)
  • Pre-order Learning Spring Boot 3.0 3rd Edition (again, me)

(What person doesn’t mention their own book, right?)

Everyone consumes information in different ways. Whether that’s

5 — Check out some videos

There are multiple YouTube channels where you start learning Spring coding in video format.

  • Dan Vega (Spring Developer Advocate)
  • Josh Long (Spring Developer Advocate)
  • Spring Boot Learning (me!)

All these videos are 100% free. Check ’em out to see who if they suit your needs.

6 — Carve out some space to start CODING!

This may sound a bit weird, but if you want to be a pro coder in the land of Spring, then find a place in your home or apartment. Clean it out and make space.

Don’t just sit around at the dining table or in your living room chair.

No, find a REAL place to dedicate to writing code. It could be as simple as a small IKEA desk in the corner of a room…or a standing desk if you want. Making a dedicated space is the first step of commitment to start growing your skills.

Then…start coding. Use the guides, the books and the videos mentioned above. Whatever it takes.

Because one thing this all requires…is YOU!

YOU must commit to digging in and learning.
